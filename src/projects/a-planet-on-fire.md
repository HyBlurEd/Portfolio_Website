## Game Design

The core challenge: **convert real wildfire suppression tactics into game mechanics** that are both educational and engaging.

- **Level design** — vehicle-based combat arenas built around fire behavior
- **Level sequence** — scenarios with a steady increase in difficulty and complexity
- **Vehicle design** — fire trucks and tools that map to real-world firefighting roles

## Game Art

All 3D assets created solo:

- **Vehicles & tools** — trucks, hoses, suppression gear
- **Environments** — forests, terrain, weather states
- **Texturing** — PBR pipeline in Unreal

## Research

The game is grounded in real-world firefighting practice:

- **Fire mechanics** — how wildfires actually spread and behave
- **Wildfire fighting** — established tactics from professional crews
- **Risks and factors** — wind, fuel, terrain, and how they interact

## Inspiration

Drawn from established frameworks in the field:

- **Transformational Framework** — applying serious-game design to real-world skills
- **Environmental Game Design** — where mechanics emerge from simulating a system
- **Tactics** — encoding procedural knowledge into player-actionable decisions

## Background

*“A Planet on Fire”* is a continuation of *“Planet on Fire”*, a smaller game I built in 2020 as an application project for the Cologne Game Lab. I wanted to recreate the original with all the new tools and methods I'd picked up during my studies — and turn it into a full BA thesis project.

*“Planet on Fire”* (the 2020 original): The fire is spreading — rescue as many trees as possible before they all burn up. Suppress fires on a planetary map. 3rd person vehicle game, 2020, Unreal Engine 4.23.
